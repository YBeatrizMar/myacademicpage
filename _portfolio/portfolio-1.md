title: "Digital Humanities Project"
excerpt: "This project proposes the creation of a digital corpus of spontaneous colloquial conversations among Spanish-speaking Cuban migrants in Miami. The study focuses on the following main questions: How do speakers manage politeness, mitigation, and identity construction in everyday interaction? What sociolinguistic and pragmalinguistic features characterize the colloquial Spanish of Cuban migrants in Miami? How do Cuban migrants discursively construct their experiences of migration, identity, ideology, and (in)adaptation? The project aims to develop an open-access, interactive corpus that integrates audio recordings, transcriptions, and sociolinguistic metadata.
Data are collected through naturalistic recordings and processed using transcription conventions adapted from the pan-Hispanic project “América y España. Español Coloquial” (AMERESCO), which brings together subcorpora from sixteen cities. The methodology employs ELAN, an annotation tool for audio alignment, enabling both qualitative and computational analysis. Additionally, AUDACITY is used to anonymize the audio files.
This project contributes not only to computational and corpus linguistics but also to the digital humanities by combining research in pragmatics and sociolinguistics with the design of digital corpora, thereby providing a resource for interdisciplinary research on language, migration, and identity in Miami."
